  • Pickleball was invented in 1965 on Bainbridge Island, near Seattle, Washington, by three fathers who wanted to create a new game for their children to play.
  • The game was named after the founder's dog, a cocker spaniel named Pickles, who would often chase after the ball during their matches.
  • Pickleball is a combination of tennis, badminton, and ping-pong. It is played on a court that is one-third the size of a tennis court.
  • The paddles used in pickleball are typically made of wood, composite materials, or graphite, and are slightly larger than the paddles used in ping-pong.
  • The ball used in pickleball is similar to a Wiffle ball, with holes on the surface to reduce its speed and increase its flight time.
  • Pickleball is a popular sport among seniors, as it is easier on the joints and requires less physical exertion than other sports like tennis.
  • The USA Pickleball Association (USAPA) was founded in 1984, and the first national tournament was held in 2009.
  • Pickleball is now played in over 5,000 locations across the United States, and it is also gaining popularity in other countries like Canada, Australia, and Spain.
  • In 2021, pickleball was added to the Olympic program for the first time, as a demonstration sport in Tokyo.
  • There are many variations of pickleball, including singles, doubles, and mixed doubles, as well as different scoring systems and rule sets.
